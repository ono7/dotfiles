### notes

most constrained variable rule

- `most constrained variable rule` solve the variable that has the most constraints, this will be the variable that has the lowest number of possible values.

- reduce, divide, restate the problem are ways to find better soluctions to a problem faster
- dividing a problem can often lower its difficulty by a magnitude
- reducing a problem eliminates choice and makes it easier to find the correct solution(s)
- if you cant find division to a problem reduce it
- once its broken down into pieces, start by solving parts that you already know how to solve to make progress right away. having a working partial solution may give more ideas about solving the rest of the problem.
- start with what you know also applies to cases where the problem cant or has not been divided
- try to solve problems with tools you already know before going to other tools you dont yet know. like fixing problems at home, you first try the tools in your garage before heading to the hardware store to buy new ones.

- `reduce a problem` - reduce the scope of the problem when its not solvable right away, can we solve a small part of it
  first? by adding or revoming constraints to produce a problem that we know how to solve. for example, if we have to
  solve x,y,z coordinates on a 3d space to find if two coordinates are next to each other, what if we can solve x,y
  first, or just z

- `analogies` or similarities between problems that have already been solved or can be exploited to partially solve
  the current problems.
